## Step 3: Fill Your Businesses File

**Time:** 5-15 minutes depending on how detailed you want to be

Open: `wiki/01-businesses.md`

For your main business, add:

```markdown
## [Business Name]

**What it does:** [One sentence - what the business does]

**Revenue model:** [How it makes money]

**Team:** [Key people involved]

**Key metrics:** [What success looks like]

**Status:** [Active / Growing / Scaling]

**Recent wins:** [What's working]

**Challenges:** [What's not working]
```

**Example:**
```markdown
## Max Results Media

**What it does:** Media buying for TV, radio, and streaming advertising

**Revenue model:** Commission on media placement + consulting fees

**Team:** Steve (founder), Christina (ops), Ava (executive assistant)

**Key metrics:** $20K/month net profit target, 10+ active clients

**Status:** Active, targeting 30% growth

**Recent wins:** Landed 2 Fortune 500 clients last month

**Challenges:** Scaling operations without increasing headcount
```

## Step 4: Fill Your Rules File

**Time:** 10-20 minutes depending on how many rules you document

Open: `wiki/04-rules.md`

Add your top 3-5 operating rules. Use this format for each:

```markdown
## Rule: [Rule Name]

**What it is:** [The rule itself]

**Why it matters:** [Why you follow this]

**When it applies:** [Situations where this rule kicks in]

**Exception:** [When would you break this rule?]
```

**Examples:**

```markdown
## Rule: Require 20% Value Add on All Deals

**What it is:** I won't commit to a deal unless I can prove I'm creating 20% value

**Why it matters:** Protects my time. Ensures I'm adding real value.

**When it applies:** Every acquisition, partnership, investment decision

**Exception:** Strategic deals (market entry, relationships) where long-term value justifies short-term low returns

---

## Rule: Email For Async, Calls For Decisions

**What it is:** Use email for updates/status. Use calls for decisions/complex conversations.

**Why it matters:** I can focus on strategy without constant interruptions

**When it applies:** Team communication

**Exception:** Emergencies need immediate calls

---

## Rule: Never Risk More Than 10% on Single Deal

**What it is:** No deal gets more than 10% of my capital

**Why it matters:** Diversification. Protects net worth. Sleep better.

**When it applies:** Every capital deployment decision

**Exception:** None — this is non-negotiable
```

## Pro Tips

✅ **DO:**
- Update your wiki weekly (even small changes)
- Add lessons after important projects
- Be specific in your rules (not "profitable" but "20%+ margins")
- Add new tools as you discover them
- Use your real voice in the wiki (write like you speak)

❌ **DON'T:**
- Leave wiki empty (agent needs this to work)
- Make rules you won't follow (kills credibility)
- Keep secrets from the wiki (more is better)
- Overcomplicate (start simple, add detail over time)

## Troubleshooting

**Q: I don't know what to put in my wiki**  
A: Start with what's obvious (your business name, what you do, your top rule). Add more as you go. It grows over time.

**Q: How detailed should my rules be?**  
A: Detailed enough that someone else could make decisions using your rules. Usually 2-3 sentences per rule.

**Q: What if my agent suggests something that doesn't fit my rules?**  
A: Good catch! Either: A) Update the rule (you learned something), or B) Add an exception. Document both.

**Q: How often should I update my wiki?**  
A: Weekly is ideal (10 min per week). Monthly minimum. Never leave it stale.

**Q: Can I change my rules after I document them?**  
A: Yes! Rules evolve as your business grows. Just update them and document the date.
