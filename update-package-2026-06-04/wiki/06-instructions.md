# Instructions & Procedures

Document how you do things: procedures, SOPs, workflows, best practices.

Your agent uses these to execute tasks the way you prefer.

## Format

For each procedure, include:
- What the procedure is
- When to use it
- Step-by-step steps
- Decisions to make
- What success looks like
- Common mistakes

## Template

```markdown
## Procedure: [Procedure Name]

**When to use it:** [Situations where this procedure applies]

**Purpose:** [What's the goal?]

**Steps:**
1. [First step]
2. [Second step]
3. [Third step]
...

**Decision points:** [Where do you need to make a decision? What criteria do you use?]

**Success looks like:** [How do you know if it worked?]

**Common mistakes:** [What typically goes wrong?]

**Time estimate:** [How long does this take?]

**Tools needed:** [Any tools, templates, or files required?]

**Who should do this:** [Should the agent do this? Or should it be escalated to you?]
```

---

## Example Procedure

```markdown
## Procedure: Evaluate a Real Estate Deal

**When to use it:** When someone brings you a deal to consider

**Purpose:** Quickly determine if a deal is worth pursuing

**Steps:**
1. Get the address and basic details
2. Pull comps on Zillow
3. Estimate ARV
4. Ask for seller motivation
5. Calculate your potential value add
6. Compare against your deal criteria
7. Say yes/no/maybe

**Decision points:**
- Does it meet your financial criteria?
- Is the value add achievable?
- Do you have capacity to do this deal?

**Success looks like:** You've made a clear yes/no decision, and the seller knows your answer

**Common mistakes:** Spending too long on marginal deals. Trying to force deals that don't fit your criteria.

**Time estimate:** 15-30 minutes per deal

**Tools needed:** Zillow, comparable sales data, your deal analysis spreadsheet

**Who should do this:** Agent can do preliminary evaluation, escalate final yes/no to Steve
```
