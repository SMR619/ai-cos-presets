# Your Rules & Decision Framework

Document your standing rules, operating principles, and decision frameworks.

Your agent uses these to make decisions aligned with what you care about.

## Format

For each rule, include:
- What the rule is
- Why it matters
- When it applies
- Any exceptions

## Template

```markdown
## Rule: [Rule Name]

**What it is:** [The rule itself - be specific and clear]

**Why it matters:** [Why you follow this rule - what's the principle behind it?]

**When it applies:** [Situations where this rule kicks in]

**Exception:** [Are there exceptions? Under what conditions would you break this rule?]

**How to apply it:** [How should your agent implement this rule in practice?]
```

---

## Example

```markdown
## Rule: Only accept deals where I can create 20% value add

**What it is:** No deal unless I can demonstrably improve the situation by at least 20%

**Why it matters:** Protects time from low-value deals. Ensures I'm adding real value, not just taking commission.

**When it applies:** Every deal evaluation, partnership opportunity, investment decision

**Exception:** Strategic deals (market entry, relationship building) where long-term value justifies short-term low returns

**How to apply it:** Articulate the value add in writing before committing. Show Steve the math.
```

---

## Types of Rules to Document

- **Financial rules** — What ROI/returns do you require? What's your max investment?
- **Deal rules** — What deal structures do you do? What's a dealbreaker?
- **Time rules** — What gets your time? What don't you do?
- **People rules** — Who do you work with? Who do you avoid?
- **Communication rules** — How do you want to be communicated with?
- **Risk rules** — What's your risk tolerance? What's non-negotiable?
- **Team rules** — How do you want your team to operate?
