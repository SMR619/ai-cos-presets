# Wiki — Your Domain Knowledge Base

Your personal wiki is where you organize everything you know about your businesses, deals, people, and rules. It's structured so your AI agent can instantly reference it when needed.

## What's in Your Wiki

This wiki has 6 sections:

1. **01-businesses.md** — Your business descriptions (what each business does, revenue model, key metrics)
2. **02-deals.md** — Your deal knowledge (deal types, structures, lessons learned, red flags, green lights)
3. **03-people.md** — Your people (relationships, contacts, key stakeholders, context about them)
4. **04-rules.md** — Your standing rules (how you operate, decision frameworks, what you care about)
5. **05-ies.md** — Company-specific details (Integrity Energy Systems details, if applicable)
6. **06-instructions.md** — Detailed procedures (how you do things, processes, SOPs)

Plus:
- **communication-playbook.md** — How you communicate (frameworks, tactics, your voice)
- **frameworks/** folder — All the business and communication frameworks you use

## Why This Matters

When your AI agent needs to make decisions or communicate on your behalf, it reads this wiki to understand:
- What you care about
- How you operate
- What deals look like
- Who matters
- What you've learned

**Without this wiki:** Agent has no context. Decisions are generic.  
**With this wiki:** Agent knows your business inside out. Decisions are smart and personalized.

## How to Set It Up

### For Each Business

Open **01-businesses.md** and add:

```markdown
## Business Name

**What it does:** [One sentence description]

**Revenue model:** [How it makes money]

**Team:** [Key people involved]

**Key metrics:** [What success looks like]

**Status:** [Active, growth mode, maintenance, etc.]

**Recent wins:** [What's working]

**Challenges:** [What's not working]
```

### For Deal Knowledge

Open **02-deals.md** and add:

```markdown
## Deal Structure: [Type Name]

**How it works:** [Explain the structure]

**Red flags:** [What to watch for]

**Green lights:** [What to look for]

**Example:** [A real deal you've done]

**Lessons learned:** [What you've learned from doing this]
```

### For People

Open **03-people.md** and add:

```markdown
## [Person Name]

**Relationship:** [How do you know them?]

**What they do:** [Their business/role]

**Important context:** [What your agent should know when communicating with them]

**How to work with them:** [Their preferences, communication style, what matters to them]
```

### For Your Rules

Open **04-rules.md** and add:

```markdown
## Rule: [Rule Name]

**What it is:** [The rule]

**Why it matters:** [Why you follow this]

**When it applies:** [When you use this rule]

**Exception:** [Are there exceptions?]
```

## How Your Agent Uses This

When you ask your agent to do something, it:

1. **Reads your businesses** — "What does this person/company do?"
2. **Reads your deals** — "Is this a good deal? What's the structure?"
3. **Reads your people** — "How should I communicate with this person?"
4. **Reads your rules** — "What matters to you here?"
5. **Makes a decision** — Based on YOUR context, not generic knowledge

## Example: Your Agent's First Email

Without wiki:
> "Dear Steve, I've prepared a standard business proposal for your review."

With wiki (because wiki says you prefer direct, no-fluff communication):
> "Steve - Quick summary: This deal structure works for you because [your specific criteria]. Deal for?  Let me know what questions you have."

Same task, 100% better result. That's the power of the wiki.

## Tips

- **Be specific** — More detail = smarter agent decisions
- **Update regularly** — New deal lessons? Add them. Relationship changes? Update it.
- **Use your voice** — Write like you speak. Agent will mirror your style.
- **Link to external files** — Point to your deal files, videos, research. Agent can reference them.

## What's Private?

Everything in your wiki is private to your agent. It doesn't get shared with anyone (unless you specifically ask your agent to share something).
