# Deal Tracker Module

## What This Module Does

I organize, track, and analyze your deals so nothing falls through the cracks and you always know what's working.

**What you get:**
- ✅ Organized deal folders (with templates, checklists, file organization)
- ✅ Deal status tracking (who's involved, what stage, what's next)
- ✅ Deal analysis (is this deal good? What's the ROI? Should we take it?)
- ✅ Financial tracking (deal numbers, profitability, trends)
- ✅ Follow-up management (what needs to happen next, when)
- ✅ CRM auto-sync (everything logs to GHL automatically)

**Real example:**
- You: "What deals do we have live?"
- Me: I show you all open deals, their status, who's involved, what's pending
- You: "Analyze the Brownstone deal"
- Me: I review numbers, lender terms, risk, timeline, profitability. I give you: Should we take it? What are the key risks? What's the ROI?
- You: "I got a response from the lender"
- Me: I update the deal file, log the interaction to GHL, flag next steps

## What You Need (Setup Checklist)

### Step 1: Deal Folder Structure (10 minutes)

Create this folder structure in your workspace:
```
/deals/
├── TEMPLATE-DEAL-FOLDER/ (use this as your template)
│   ├── deal-summary.md (1-page overview)
│   ├── financials/ (numbers, ROI calculations)
│   ├── parties/ (contact info, terms with each lender/borrower)
│   ├── documents/ (contracts, inspections, appraisals)
│   ├── correspondence/ (emails, responses, decisions)
│   └── status.md (current stage, timeline, next steps)
│
├── DEAL-NAME-1/
├── DEAL-NAME-2/
└── [etc.]
```

**Why:** Consistent structure means I can find what you need fast, analyze accurately, and update without confusion.

### Step 2: Deal Naming Convention (2 minutes)

Name deals clearly:
- **Good:** "BROWNSTONE-5UNIT-BROOKLYN-2026"
- **Good:** "SUBJECT-TO-KANSAS-CITY-RENTAL"
- **Avoid:** "Deal1", "Q2-Project", "Maybe"

**Why:** Makes folders searchable and clear.

### Step 3: Lender Profiles (5 minutes)

For each lender you work with, I need:
- Lender name + contact
- What they'll do (subject-to, seller financing, traditional, hard money, etc.)
- Typical terms (rates, points, timeline)
- Max loan size, minimum equity, special requirements

**You provide this once. I keep it updated.**

### Step 4: Connect Your CRM (15-30 minutes)

Choose how to sync deals:
- **Option A:** Connect GoHighLevel (recommended if no existing CRM)
- **Option B:** Connect your existing CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, Pipedrive, etc.)
- **Option C:** Manual tracking (no CRM integration)

See CRM_SYNC module for detailed setup instructions.

### Step 5: Ready to Use

You can now:
- Ask me to organize a new deal
- Ask me to analyze a deal
- Track deal status
- Get financial summaries
- Know ROI and profitability
- Auto-log to your CRM (if connected)

## How I Work (Agent Guidelines)

### Core Principle

When you tell me about a deal, I:
1. **Organize it** — create deal folder, gather documents, organize parties
2. **Analyze it** — review numbers, compare to lender terms, assess risk
3. **Track it** — update status, log interactions, flag next steps
4. **Improve decisions** — show you ROI, profitability, risk so you decide fast

### Deal Analysis: What I Look At

**Financial:**
- Purchase price / equity invested
- Repair costs and timeline
- After-repair value (ARV)
- Holding costs (carrying cost, insurance, taxes)
- Exit strategy (wholesale, retail, hold, refinance)
- **Result:** ROI, cash-on-cash return, profit

**Lender Fit:**
- Does this lender do this TYPE of deal? (subject-to, hard money, etc.)
- Does it meet their SIZE requirements? (min/max loan)
- Does it meet their TERMS requirements? (equity, rates, timeline)
- **Result:** Will they fund it? What are the terms?

**Risk:**
- Is the property good? (condition, location, market)
- Is the ARV estimate realistic?
- Are there exit options if something goes wrong?
- What could go wrong? (market, repairs, lender issues)
- **Result:** Safe or risky? How much buffer?

**Timeline:**
- When do you need funding?
- How long will repairs take?
- When's the exit?
- **Result:** Is timing realistic?

### Financial Tracking: What I Show You

**Per Deal:**
- Total invested (down payment + repairs + holding)
- Projected profit (ARV - total costs)
- Timeline (acquisition to exit)
- ROI % and cash-on-cash return

**Across All Deals:**
- Portfolio overview (how many deals, total invested, total expected profit)
- Average ROI across deals
- Best-performing deals (what's working)
- Deals at risk (what needs attention)
- Trends (are we improving?)
