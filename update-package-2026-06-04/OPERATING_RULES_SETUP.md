# Operating Rules — Setup & How They Work

Operating rules are your decision-making framework. They tell your agent (and your team) how you want things done.

---

## What Are Operating Rules?

**Operating rules** = The principles and constraints that govern how you operate

They answer questions like:
- "What financial returns do I require?"
- "What kind of deals do I do?"
- "How much risk can I take?"
- "What won't I compromise on?"
- "How do I want my team to operate?"

---

## Why Operating Rules Matter

**Without rules:** Agent makes assumptions. Team guesses at your preferences. Decisions are inconsistent.  
**With rules:** Agent knows how you want things done. Team operates autonomously because they know the boundaries.

---

## Types of Rules

### Financial Rules
How much capital do you deploy? What returns do you require? What's your max risk per deal?

**Examples:**
- "Require 20% IRR minimum on all deals"
- "Max capital per deal: $500K"
- "Prefer cash-flowing assets over appreciation plays"
- "Accept 12-month payback period for real estate"

### Deal Rules
What deal structures do you do? What's a dealbreaker?

**Examples:**
- "Only do subject-to deals with 20%+ equity"
- "No deal with more than 2 exit strategies"
- "Must be able to speak to the decision-maker within 24 hours"
- "Avoid deals in declining markets"

### Time & Capacity Rules
What gets your time? What doesn't?

**Examples:**
- "Decisions over $100K require my direct review"
- "No new businesses until Q4"
- "Max 2 new projects per quarter"
- "Weekly 1-on-1s with each team member"

### People & Team Rules
How do you want your team to operate?

**Examples:**
- "Make decisions up to $10K autonomously, escalate above that"
- "Document everything — no tribal knowledge"
- "Ask for permission on PR, deny on details"
- "Hire for culture first, skills second"

### Communication Rules
How do you want to be communicated with?

**Examples:**
- "Email for async, Slack for urgent, calls for decisions"
- "Weekly summary on Fridays, not daily updates"
- "I prefer data over opinions"
- "Tell me what you recommend, not just options"

### Risk Rules
What's non-negotiable? What can you tolerate?

**Examples:**
- "No personal guarantees on debt"
- "Always have 6 months runway"
- "Never risk 50% of net worth on one deal"
- "Diversify across asset classes"

## Setting Up Your Operating Rules

### Step 1: Identify Your Top Rules
What 5-10 rules would you want your team to know about you?

Start with:
1. **Financial criteria** — What returns/cash flow do you want?
2. **Deal criteria** — What do you buy/do?
3. **Risk tolerance** — What's your comfort level?
4. **Time allocation** — What gets your time?
5. **Communication preference** — How do you want to communicate?

### Step 2: Document Each Rule Clearly

Use this format:
```markdown
## Rule: [Rule Name]

**What it is:** [The rule itself]

**Why:** [Why you follow this rule]

**When it applies:** [What situations trigger this rule]

**Exception:** [Are there exceptions? When would you break this rule?]

**How to apply:** [How should someone implement this?]
```

### Step 3: Share With Your Team & Agent
Your rules aren't secret. They should guide:
- Team decisions
- Your agent's recommendations
- Deal evaluations
- Resource allocation

---

## Template: Writing a Rule

```markdown
## Rule: Require 20% Value Add on Every Deal

**What it is:** I won't commit to a deal unless I can demonstrably create 20% value

**Why:** Protects my time. Ensures I'm adding real value, not just taking commission. Builds reputation.

**When it applies:** Every acquisition, partnership, or major deal

**Exception:** Strategic deals (market entry, relationship building) where long-term value justifies short-term low returns

**How to apply:** 
- Articulate the value add before committing
- Get it in writing
- Run the numbers
- If you can't hit 20%, bring it to me before saying yes
```

## Rule Examples

### Real Estate
```markdown
## Rule: Subject-to Deals Only Below Asking Price

**What:** Only do subject-to deals where property is 15%+ below market

**Why:** Ensures equity from day one. Protects against market downturns.

**When:** Property acquisitions

**Exception:** Strategic properties in high-growth markets
```

### Team Management
```markdown
## Rule: Hiring Decision Requires 3 Interviews

**What:** No offer without 3 separate interviews with different people

**Why:** Catches culture mismatch. Different people see different strengths. Team buy-in.

**When:** All hiring

**Exception:** None — this is non-negotiable
```

### Communication
```markdown
## Rule: Weekly Summaries, Not Daily Updates

**What:** I want one email per week with what happened, decisions made, what's next

**Why:** I can focus on strategy. Reduces noise. Cleaner decision-making.

**When:** Team communication

**Exception:** Emergencies (decision needed, problem, major risk) — those are urgent
```

## How Your Agent Uses Rules

### When evaluating a deal:
Agent checks your financial rules, deal rules, risk rules
→ "Does this fit Steve's criteria?"

### When making a decision:
Agent checks your decision rules
→ "Is this mine to decide, or Steve's?"

### When communicating:
Agent checks your communication rules
→ "Email or call? Summary or details?"

### When recommending strategy:
Agent checks your risk rules and time rules
→ "Is this aligned with Steve's risk tolerance and capacity?"

## Updating Your Rules

Your rules can change as you grow:

- **Quarterly review** — Do these rules still feel right?
- **When things change** — New business? New market? Update rules.
- **When you violate a rule** — Usually means you need a new exception, OR the rule needs updating

**Log changes with dates:** "Rule updated 2026-06-04 because..."

---

## Pro Tips

✅ **DO:**
- Be specific (not "be profitable" but "20%+ margins")
- Be honest (don't write rules you won't follow)
- Link to examples (show how rule applies)
- Share openly (team should know your rules)
- Update regularly (rules evolve as you grow)

❌ **DON'T:**
- Make rules you won't follow (credibility killer)
- Make rules too vague (no room for interpretation)
- Make too many rules (focus on the important ones)
- Hide your rules (they should guide everyone)
- Never update them (business changes, rules should too)

## The Power of Clear Rules

**What happens with clear operating rules:**
- Team makes decisions aligned with you
- Agent recommends actions you'd approve
- Fewer decisions to make personally
- Faster execution
- Better deals/outcomes (because they're filtered by your criteria)

**What happens without clear rules:**
- Team guesses at your preferences
- Agent makes assumptions
- Everything escalates to you
- Slow decision-making
- Inconsistent outcomes

## Questions?

**"What if I break my own rule?"**  
Answer: Document why. You learned something. Update the rule or add an exception.

**"What if my rule is too restrictive?"**  
Answer: Test it for a quarter. If you keep wanting to break it, change it.

**"What if rules conflict?"**  
Answer: That's a decision point. Document the priority. How do you decide when rules compete?

---

## Integration with Your Agent

Your agent will:
- Know your rules from day one
- Use them to evaluate opportunities
- Escalate decisions outside your rules
- Remind you of your rules if you're considering something that violates them
- Help you iterate rules as your business grows

**Result: Autonomous execution within your framework.**

**Clear operating rules = Scaling lever**
