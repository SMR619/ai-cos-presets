# Frameworks Guide — Communication & Business

Your frameworks are proven patterns for thinking, communicating, and solving problems.

Your agent uses these to structure decisions, communications, and strategies.

---

## What Are Frameworks?

A **framework** is a repeatable pattern that works.

**Example:** The "Problem-Agitate-Solution" (PAS) framework shows how to write a compelling pitch:
1. Identify the problem the customer has
2. Make them feel the pain (agitate)
3. Show how you solve it (solution)

Same framework works for sales emails, landing pages, presentations, and investor pitches.

---

## Your Framework Library

Your `frameworks/` folder contains frameworks organized by type:

### Communication Frameworks
How to persuade, tell stories, influence people, negotiate

**Examples:**
- Tactical Empathy (understand the other person's position)
- Storytelling (hero's journey, specific story formulas)
- LAPS Sales System (listen, affirm, pivot, solve)
- Frameworks for cold email, warm outreach, negotiation

### Business Frameworks
How to structure business models, pricing, lead generation, growth

**Examples:**
- 100M Offers (how to structure an irresistible offer)
- 100M Leads (lead generation systems)
- Pricing frameworks (value-based, cost-plus, etc.)
- Deal structuring

### Operations Frameworks
How to scale, build teams, systematize, delegate

**Examples:**
- EOS (Entrepreneurial Operating System)
- E-Myth (systems and delegation)
- Traction (scaling framework)

### Financial Frameworks
How to analyze deals, structure financing, calculate ROI

**Examples:**
- Deal analysis (cap rates, cash-on-cash, etc.)
- LTV and unit economics
- Financing options and structures

### Build/Technical Frameworks
How to develop software, apps, and technical systems

## How Your Agent Uses Frameworks

### When writing a sales email:
Agent thinks: "This is a cold outreach email. Let me use the PAS framework + LAPS system... Problem (they're losing deals), Agitate (costing them $X/month), Solution (our system prevents this)"

### When structuring an offer:
Agent thinks: "Let me check the 100M Offers framework... what's the transformation? What's the deliverable? What's the price point that makes sense?"

### When advising on a team issue:
Agent thinks: "This is a scaling problem. Let me check the E-Myth framework and EOS framework... here's how to systematize this..."

## How to Find the Right Framework

**By problem:**
- "I need to write a persuasive email" → Look in Communication Frameworks
- "I need to price this service" → Look in Business Frameworks
- "I need to scale my operations" → Look in Operations Frameworks
- "I need to structure this deal" → Look in Financial Frameworks

**By situation:**
- Negotiating with someone → Tactical Empathy
- Selling something new → 100M Offers + PAS framework
- Building a team → EOS + E-Myth
- Analyzing a deal → Financial frameworks

**Ask your agent:** "Which framework applies to this situation?"

## Adding New Frameworks

When you learn a new framework (from a book, video, training, real experience):

1. **Add it to the appropriate file** in `frameworks/`
2. **Document the framework** — Name, what it does, how to use it, when to apply it
3. **Add an example** — Show how it works in practice
4. **Tag it** — Mark key applications so you can find it later

Example:
```markdown
## Framework: The VCP Process

**What it is:** Know, Like, Trust ladder for relationship building

**How it works:**
1. Visibility — Get in front of the person
2. Credibility — Prove you know what you're talking about
3. Profitability — Show there's a mutual benefit

**When to use it:** Building relationships with investors, partners, high-value contacts

**Example:** [Real example of how you've applied this]

**Key insight:** Most people skip the visibility and credibility steps and go straight to "here's what I want" — doesn't work
```

## Framework Combinations

Powerful combinations often work better than single frameworks:

**Cold Outreach:**
- PAS Framework (structure) + LAPS System (conversation flow) + Tactical Empathy (understanding their position)

**Sales Pitch:**
- 100M Offers (structure) + Storytelling (engagement) + Evidence (proof)

**Negotiation:**
- Tactical Empathy (understand their needs) + Interest-Based Relational Approach (find mutual gains)

## Pro Tips

✅ **DO:**
- Use frameworks as thinking tools, not formulas
- Adapt frameworks to your situation
- Combine frameworks for more power
- Add frameworks you discover
- Use them with your agent (reference by name)

❌ **DON'T:**
- Follow frameworks rigidly (they're guides, not rules)
- Use a framework when it doesn't fit
- Ignore the "why" behind the framework
- Assume one framework works for everything

## Examples of Frameworks in Action

### Example 1: Cold Email Outreach

**Situation:** You need to reach out to a potential partner

**Agent uses:**
1. PAS Framework → structure the email (problem, agitate, solution)
2. LAPS System → what to say in conversation
3. Your rules → what value-add to promise

**Result:** Personalized, compelling email that respects the recipient and positions you well

### Example 2: Deal Analysis

**Situation:** Someone brings you a potential real estate deal

**Agent uses:**
1. Your deal frameworks → what structures you do
2. Financial frameworks → analyzing ROI, cap rates, cash-on-cash
3. Your rules → 20% value-add minimum?

**Result:** Agent provides analysis aligned with how you think and what you care about

### Example 3: Team Communication

**Situation:** You need to explain a new process to your team

**Agent uses:**
1. Operations frameworks (EOS, E-Myth) → how to systematize
2. Your communication style → your voice, directness level
3. Your rules → what decisions are yours, what's delegated

**Result:** Clear, actionable communication that your team understands
